Our Story

This work started as love. Now it helps families like yours.

When a close friend's grandmother, Neoma, passed, she left behind a lifetime of cherished belongings, scattered documents, and an estate that felt impossible to sort through while grieving. That's when I stepped in — not as a professional, but as someone who cared.

What I discovered was a gap no one talks about: the overwhelming administrative reality that hits a family right when they're at their most vulnerable. Insurance policies buried in closets. Property deeds mixed in with holiday cards. No single organized record of what existed, where it was, or what it was worth.

Neoma's Legacy Treasures was built to fill that gap. What started as doing a favor for a grieving friend has grown into a formal service that brings calm, structure, and professionalism to one of the most difficult seasons a family can face.

The Framework

The Four Pillars of a
Well-Organized Legacy

Every estate engagement is built around four domains that together form a complete, navigable picture of a loved one's life and assets.

01

Essential Identity

The foundational documents that establish who someone was — legally and personally.

  • Birth & death certificates
  • Social Security documentation
  • Will, trust & legal directives
  • Passports & government IDs
  • Marriage & divorce records
02

Financial Records

A clear accounting of accounts, debts, benefits, and ongoing financial obligations.

  • Bank & investment accounts
  • Life insurance & beneficiaries
  • Pension & retirement funds
  • Outstanding debts & loans
  • Tax filings & employer records
03

Property & Assets

Cataloging the physical and digital estate — from real property to valuables and collectibles.

  • Real estate deeds & mortgages
  • Vehicle titles & registrations
  • Business interests & assets
  • Collectibles & valuables inventory
  • Digital accounts & subscriptions
04

Legacy Letters

Preserving the personal story — the wishes, memories, and meaning behind the material.

  • Ethical will & personal messages
  • Funeral & memorial preferences
  • Family history & keepsakes notes
  • Charitable & gifting intentions
  • Final wishes & contacts list
